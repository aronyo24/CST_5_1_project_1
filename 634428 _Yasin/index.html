Kingfishers are a family, the Alcedinidae, of small to medium-sized, brightly colored birds in the order Coraciiformes.ll kingfishers have large heads, long, sharp, pointed bills, short legs, and stubby tails. Most species have bright plumage with only small differences between the sexes. Most species are tropical in distribution, and a slight majority are found only in forests.
The bulbuls are members of a family, Pycnonotidae, of medium-sized passerine songbirds, which also includes greenbuls, brownbuls, leafloves, and bristlebills.Its feathers are long, soft and puffy. Lips short to medium and slightly curved. The legs are thin and weak. The wings are short and rounded. It has hairy feathers on its neck and developed cartilage around the anus. The tail is relatively long.Bulbuls live in gardens, farmlands and woodlands. Their main food is fruits, flower extracts and insects.
